About Us

Meet the Owner

Cat Plesko

The Jacksonville Pottery Studio is owned by Cat Plesko, also the owner of Cat Scratch Ceramics. Cat has known she wanted to open up a pottery studio in Jacksonville as soon as she started her career in the arts. She started off taking a pottery class in her home town Tampa, at the Academy of the Holy Names High School. She instantly knew this was her calling but she also learned as she went on to college her love of business and management. Cat started her degree in management, but it was her love of being an athlete rower and ceramicists where she found herself transferring to Jacksonville University.

Cat took on an internship in 2014 to work for the Public Art Sector of the Cultural Council and fell in love with the management part of the arts. She then took on teaching throwing classes at a local non profit art center and made her way with Cat Scratch in selling her work at local markets, but her love was really in spreading her love of teaching and she knew Jacksonville was in need of a Pottery studio.

This is when Jacksonville Pottery Studio was born. Jacksonville Pottery Studio is a community space to make and share the love the of the craft of pottery with others.

The Jacksonville Pottery Studio is a pottery studio with weekly classes on how to make pottery and a shared community center for members to use the space to make their very own creations.

Meet the Instructors

Megan Reid

Originally born in Potsdam, Germany, Megan has lived all over the United States and now calls the bold city of Jacksonville, Florida home. She received her degree from Jacksonville University with a double concentration in Ceramics and Glass. She is passionate about the arts and is a practicing visual artist in several media. Megan's art is a reflection of her interest in the natural world surrounding her. Megan is also a registered yoga teacher and says that the meditative practice helps to further inspire her designs. Megan’s sculptures can be found in a few local galleries. She is extremely thankful for her opportunities in the arts and is an advocate for giving back to the community.Megan is a volunteer at the nonprofit Word Revolt Art Gallery and local beach cleanup efforts. She is well connected in the Jacksonville and Beaches area. Megan says, "The arts are especially crucial during this pandemic to uplift morale and usher in a new era. I am proud to serve the artists and community through my role at the Jacksonville Pottery Studio

Cal Cook

Jacksonville native and neighborhood face, Cal Cook is an artist and educator who has been teaching ceramics through local art centers since 2017. He graduated from UNF in 2015 with his BFA in studio ceramics, acquiring a balanced skill set of wheel throwing, hand building, and mold making. Now a secondary teacher in Green Cove Springs, Cal works closely with grades 9-12; helping hone their 2D art skills and art history knowledge.

Outside of teaching, you can find Cal spending time making work in his home studio, tackling a home renovation project, or delving into one of his many hobbies. A fragrance enthusiast, Cal enjoys experimenting with olfactory concepts through perfume and candle making. His favorite scent profiles are those that are earthy, green, and often polarizing. A jack of all mediums, Cal also occasionally dabbles in photographic processes, 2D design, and textile work.

As far as personal work and style, Cal’s ceramics are made with function in mind as he caters to utility and necessity by way of porcelain and stonewares. His concepts are simple, with works featuring quiet colors and haphazard imagery concerning mundane yet relatable sentiments. He enjoys making lumpy mugs, things that hold flowers, and things that hold food. You can find him and his work on Instagram @calcookceramics.

Meet the Studio Assistants

Alexa Smith

I started my ceramics journey in elementary school with my first coil pot. It didn’t take me long to figure out I could make tiny replicas of things around me using polymer clay and bake them in my parent’s toaster oven. I was reintroduced to pottery in high school where I became interested in replicating natural textures using handbuilt forms and slip decoration techniques. While managing University of Maryland’s ceramic studio, I discovered my love of glaze chemistry, a deep appreciation for a working pug mill, and the satisfaction of a tightly packed kiln. Most importantly, I witnessed how playing with clay both relieved stress and brought people together in a humbling and quite literally “down to earth” way. Although I pursued a career as a Fire Protection Engineer after graduation, I could never quite keep my hands out of clay for too long. While I consider myself a hand-builder at heart- I am the proud owner of a 1972 Shimpo RK-2 wheel- affectionately named “Tina”. I have always felt that clay is one of the most fun and stress relieving mediums to work with, and have loved sharing that joy with other people over my six years of working in various pottery studios.

Zoe Conrad

Being born and raised in Jacksonville, Florida made me a beach baby at heart. I attended Fletcher High-school and that is wear my love for ceramics was born. Hand building was the foundation of my journey. After high school I attended Florida State College at Jacksonville on a full ceramics scholarship. There Learning how to throw on the wheel, expanding my hand building skills, and appreciating the behind the scenes as a studio assistant on campus. After graduating I moved to Orlando to start my ceramics career. There I taught hand building and wheel throwing to Kindergarten through 5th grade for 2 1/2 years. But the beach called me home and who could say no to that. Now a proud studio assistant and children’s teacher at Jacksonville Pottery. My work is constantly changing like the tides, but is always inspired in some way by the natural world around us. You can see what I’m up to currently on Instagram @thepotteryprincess !
